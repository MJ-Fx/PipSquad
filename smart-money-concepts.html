Institutional Trading Secrets

Smart Money Concepts (SMC) reveal how banks, hedge funds, and institutional traders manipulate markets to their advantage. These strategies account for why 90% of retail traders lose money while the "smart money" consistently profits.

Retail Traders

  • Follow indicators with lag
  • Trade without understanding liquidity
  • Get stopped out repeatedly
  • Chase price after moves happen

Smart Money

  • Trade based on order flow
  • Target liquidity pools for entries
  • Hunt retail stop losses
  • Anticipate moves before they happen

Core Principle

"Smart money trades against retail traders by targeting areas where the majority have their stop losses and entries, creating false breakouts to sweep liquidity before reversing price in their intended direction."

Liquidity Trading

What is Liquidity?

Liquidity refers to how easily an asset can be bought or sold without significantly affecting its price. Smart money targets specific liquidity levels to enter and exit large positions.

Why Liquidity Matters to Smart Money:

  • Quick Execution: Enter/exit large positions rapidly
  • Lower Costs: Minimize slippage and transaction fees
  • Risk Management: Better control over position sizing
  • Manipulation: Easier to move price to target levels

2 Types of Liquidity

Sell Side Liquidity (SSL)

Smart Money Action: BUYS at SSL

Retail Traders: Have stop losses (longs) and short entries here

Result: Price reverses upward after SSL sweep

Buy Side Liquidity (BSL)

Smart Money Action: SELLS at BSL

Retail Traders: Have stop losses (shorts) and long entries here

Result: Price reverses downward after BSL sweep

Order Blocks

What Are Order Blocks?

Order blocks are price zones where large institutional orders were executed, causing significant moves. These become future support/resistance areas where price is likely to react again.

Bullish Order Block

Formation: Large bullish candle(s) followed by pullback

Smart Money Action: Institutional buying zone

Trade Setup: Look for longs when price returns to this zone

Bearish Order Block

Formation: Large bearish candle(s) followed by pullback

Smart Money Action: Institutional selling zone

Trade Setup: Look for shorts when price returns to this zone

Market Structure

Understanding Market Structure

Market structure shows the underlying trend and helps identify potential reversals or continuations.

Up Structure

Price makes higher highs and higher lows

Down Structure

Price makes lower highs and lower lows

Market Structure Shifts

Key changes in market structure that signal potential trend reversals or continuations:

Break of Structure (BOS)

Type: Continuation

Formation: Price breaks previous high/low in trend direction

Action: Look for trades in direction of break

Change of Character (CHoCH)

Type: Reversal

Formation: Price breaks opposite of current trend

Action: Look for trades in new direction

Fair Value Gaps

What Are Fair Value Gaps?

Fair Value Gaps (FVGs) are 3-candle patterns that show market imbalances where price is expected to return to "fill the gap" and rebalance.

Bullish FVG

Formation: Large bullish candle between two candles that don't overlap in price

Expectation: Price will drop to fill the gap (70-80% probability)

Trading: Look for short opportunities into the gap

Bearish FVG

Formation: Large bearish candle between two candles that don't overlap in price

Expectation: Price will rise to fill the gap (70-80% probability)

Trading: Look for long opportunities into the gap

Fair Value Gap Trading Rules

  1. FVGs work best on higher timeframes (1H, 4H, Daily)
  2. Stronger when aligned with liquidity levels and order blocks
  3. Price typically fills 50-100% of the gap before continuing
  4. Use in conjunction with market structure for confirmation
  5. More reliable when volume confirms the imbalance

Market Equilibrium

Understanding Equilibrium

Equilibrium represents fair value where supply and demand are balanced. Price tends to oscillate between premium (overbought) and discount (oversold) relative to equilibrium.

Premium (Above 50%)
Equilibrium (50%)
Discount (Below 50%)

Premium Market

Condition: Price trades above equilibrium (50% level)

Smart Money Action: Looks to sell at premium prices

Trading: Watch for reversals back to equilibrium

Discount Market

Condition: Price trades below equilibrium (50% level)

Smart Money Action: Looks to buy at discount prices

Trading: Watch for reversals back to equilibrium

Equilibrium Trading Strategy

1

Identify Swing Points

Mark the most recent significant swing high and swing low

2

Draw Equilibrium

Use Fibonacci or Gann Box to mark the 50% level between swing points

3

Watch for Extremes

Monitor when price reaches premium (above 50%) or discount (below 50%) zones

4

Trade the Reversion

Look for entries as price returns to equilibrium with confirmation

ICT Kill Zones

Definition

ICT Kill Zones refer to specific time periods when trading volume and market volatility are at their peak. These windows provide ideal trading opportunities, allowing traders to capitalize on the increased market activity to maximize their profits.

Explanation

We all know forex market operates 24 hours a day but not all hours offer the same volatility and trading opportunities. To fix the issue & to save traders time, Michael Huddleston also known as ICT, introduced the concept of ICT kill zones which refers to hot trading hours in the forex markets with most volatility & profitable trading opportunities.

To understand and trade using ICT kill zones, you should first set your time zone to New York local time UTC-4 and in case of day light saving UTC-5 as we will be using New York local time to explain ICT kill zones.

Note: You should also have understanding of ICT Optimal Trade Entry ‚Äì OTE Pattern before diving deep into ICT kill zones.

Kill Zones Division

ICT kill zones are divided into four periods:

  1. ICT Asian Kill Zone (08:00 PM ‚Äì 10:00 PM)
  2. ICT London Kill Zone (02:00 AM ‚Äì 05:00 AM)
  3. ICT New York Kill Zone (07:00 AM ‚Äì 09:00 AM)
  4. ICT London Close Kill Zone (10:00 AM ‚Äì 12:00 PM)

ICT Asian Kill Zone

The ICT Asian Kill Zone is the first strategic period in the forex market, focusing on the Australian Dollar (AUD), New Zealand Dollar (NZD), and Japanese Yen (JPY). These pairs are most active during this time, while the US Dollar often consolidates.

Characteristics:
  • Timing: Between 8:00 PM and 10:00 PM
  • Higher Time Frame Bias: Helpful for identifying trends, but short-term retracements (bullish or bearish) can also provide optimal trade entries
  • Scalping Opportunities: Often sets up trades that can yield 15‚Äì20 pips
  • Cross Pairs: Most setups involve cross pairs due to US Dollar consolidation
Example:

In the AUD/JPY five-minute chart with a bullish daily bias, the price retraced to the optimal trade entry level at 0.786. A buy trade was executed, targeting the previous high, successfully delivering 25 pips.

ICT London Kill Zone

The ICT London Kill Zone starts with the opening of the London session and experiences the highest trading volume compared to other sessions. The Euro (EUR) and British Pound (GBP) are the most actively traded pairs during this period.

Characteristics:
  • Timing: Between 2:00 AM and 5:00 AM
  • Higher Time Frame Bias: Useful for identifying trends
  • Directional Trades: Often sets up trades that can yield more than 30 pips
  • Bullish Days: Price typically grabs the Asian high and then creates the high of the day before declining
  • Bearish Days: Price typically grabs the Asian low and then creates the low of the day before rising
Example:

In the GBP/USD chart with a bearish daily bias, the price took the high of the Asian session, shifted market structure downward, and retraced to the optimal trade entry level at 0.5. This provided a sell opportunity, targeting the previous session's low, and successfully delivered 40 pips.

ICT New York Kill Zone

ICT New York kill zone is the opening time of New York session and during this period of time pairs coupled with US dollars are important to watch and trade.

Characteristics:
  • Timing: Between 7:00 AM and 9:00 AM
  • Higher Time Frame Bias: Useful for identifying trends, but short-term retracements (bullish or bearish) can also provide optimal trade entries
  • Retracement to London Range: The New York session often retraces to the London trading range, setting up trades that can yield 30‚Äì40 pips
  • High Volatility and Volume: Due to the overlap between the London and New York sessions
Example:

In the USD/CAD chart with a bearish daily bias, the price retraced to the London range during the New York Kill Zone. This created a selling opportunity at the optimal trade entry level of 0.786, successfully delivering 40 pips.

ICT London Close Kill Zone

The ICT London Close Kill Zone occurs when the London session closes, typically a less volatile period. During this time, prices often retrace to their daily range, offering optimal trade entry setups.

Characteristics:
  • Timing: Between 10:00 AM and 12:00 PM
  • Scalping Opportunities: Can offer five-minute optimal trade entry (OTE) setups, yielding 15‚Äì20 pips
  • Bullish Days: After making the high of the day, the price retraces back to the daily range
  • Bearish Days: After making the low of the day, the price retraces back to the daily range
Example:

In the GBP/USD chart, after making the high of the day, the price retraced back to the daily range during the London Close Kill Zone. This created a sell opportunity at the optimal trade entry level of 0.618, successfully delivering 20 pips.

ICT Daily Bias

Definition

The Daily Bias refers to the predominant market direction identified through higher time frame analysis, which helps traders align their intraday trades with the broader market trend for higher probability setups.

Explanation

ICT teaches that identifying the Daily Bias is crucial before looking for trade setups during Kill Zones. The Daily Bias is determined by analyzing:

  • Weekly and Daily chart structure
  • Market maker buy/sell models
  • Liquidity pools and order blocks
  • Fibonacci retracement levels

A proper Daily Bias identification allows traders to:

  • Filter out counter-trend trades
  • Focus on high-probability setups
  • Improve risk-reward ratios

Types of Daily Bias

Bullish Daily Bias

Identified when:

  • Price is making higher highs and higher lows
  • Recent swing low holds as support
  • Price reacts positively to key Fibonacci levels (especially 0.618 or 0.786)
  • Order blocks show accumulation patterns
Trading Implications:
  • Look for buy setups during Kill Zones
  • Focus on taking trades at support levels
  • Use bearish moves as opportunities to buy at better prices
Example:

In EUR/USD daily chart showing consecutive higher lows, with price holding above a key order block and reacting positively at 0.786 Fib level - establishes Bullish Daily Bias.

Bearish Daily Bias

Identified when:

  • Price is making lower highs and lower lows
  • Recent swing high holds as resistance
  • Price reacts negatively to key Fibonacci levels (especially 0.382 or 0.5)
  • Order blocks show distribution patterns
Trading Implications:
  • Look for sell setups during Kill Zones
  • Focus on taking trades at resistance levels
  • Use bullish moves as opportunities to sell at better prices
Example:

In GBP/USD daily chart showing rejection from a liquidity pool with consecutive lower highs, establishing Bearish Daily Bias - leading to successful sell setups during London Kill Zone.

Neutral/Ranging Daily Bias

Identified when:

  • Price oscillates between clear support and resistance
  • No clear higher highs/lows or lower highs/lows
  • Price reacts at both extremes of the range
  • Equal highs/lows form at range boundaries
Trading Implications:
  • Trade reversals at range extremes
  • Reduce position sizes
  • Take profits quicker at opposite range boundary
  • Avoid trading mid-range
Example:

USD/JPY oscillating between 109.50 support and 110.30 resistance for multiple days, creating Neutral Daily Bias - profitable reversal trades at these levels during Kill Zones.

Confirming Daily Bias

ICT recommends these confirmation techniques:

  1. Liquidity Analysis: Check for stops above/below recent swings
  2. Time of Day: Strongest confirmation often comes during London Kill Zone
  3. FVG Alignment: Fair Value Gaps aligning with bias direction
  4. Volume Profile: Higher volume in direction of bias
