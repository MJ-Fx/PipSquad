What is Price Action Trading?

Price action trading involves analyzing and trading based on the behavior of price technically without using any indicators. You're looking at:

  • Candlestick behavior and patterns
  • Market structures and formations
  • Supply/Demand areas (liquidity zones)
  • Multi-timeframe analysis

Core Principle

"There's nothing new in the markets - history repeats itself." Price action traders look for repeating patterns that indicate probable future movements.

Advantages of Price Action

  • Works on any market and timeframe
  • Eliminates lag from indicators
  • Shows what institutions are actually doing
  • Provides clear entry/exit signals

Challenges

  • Requires screen time to recognize patterns
  • Subjective interpretation needed
  • Needs combined with risk management

Candlestick Patterns

Japanese candlesticks reveal market sentiment through their shapes and formations. Each candle tells a story of the battle between buyers and sellers.

Candlestick Anatomy

High Close Open Low
High Open Close Low

Body: Represents the open-close range. Green/white = close above open (bullish). Red/black = close below open (bearish).

Shadows/Wicks: Show the high-low range. Long upper shadow = rejection of higher prices. Long lower shadow = rejection of lower prices.

Size Matters: Long bodies indicate strong buying/selling pressure. Small bodies show indecision.

Bullish Reversal Patterns

These patterns form after downtrends, signaling potential upward reversals:

Hammer

Forms at bottoms with small body and long lower shadow. Shows sellers pushed price down but buyers regained control.

Rules:
  1. Appears during downtrend
  2. Little/no upper shadow
  3. Lower shadow ≥ 2x body height
  4. Confirmation next candle

Bullish Engulfing

A small bearish candle completely engulfed by a large bullish candle. Shows overwhelming buying pressure.

Rules:
  1. First candle is bearish
  2. Second candle opens below first close
  3. Second candle closes above first open
  4. Stronger with high volume

Morning Star

3-candle pattern: long bearish, small indecision candle, then long bullish. Signals downtrend exhaustion.

Rules:
  1. First candle is long bearish
  2. Second candle gaps down (star)
  3. Third candle closes into first body
  4. Better with volume confirmation

Bearish Reversal Patterns

These patterns form after uptrends, signaling potential downward reversals:

Hanging Man

Appears at tops with small body and long lower shadow. Shows buyers losing control to sellers.

Rules:
  1. Forms after uptrend
  2. Little/no upper shadow
  3. Lower shadow ≥ 2x body
  4. Requires bearish confirmation

Bearish Engulfing

A small bullish candle completely engulfed by a large bearish candle. Shows aggressive selling.

Rules:
  1. First candle is bullish
  2. Second candle opens above first close
  3. Second candle closes below first open
  4. More significant at resistance

Evening Star

3-candle pattern: long bullish, small indecision candle, then long bearish. Shows uptrend exhaustion.

Rules:
  1. First candle is long bullish
  2. Second candle gaps up (star)
  3. Third candle closes into first body
  4. Stronger at key resistance

Continuation Patterns

These patterns indicate the existing trend will likely resume after consolidation:

Rising Wedge

Converging upward-sloping lines with steeper support. Typically breaks downward to continue bear trend.

Rules:
  1. At least 5 touch points (2 resistance, 3 support)
  2. Volume declines during formation
  3. Break below support with increased volume
  4. Price target ≈ height of wedge

Falling Wedge

Converging downward-sloping lines with steeper resistance. Typically breaks upward to continue bull trend.

Rules:
  1. At least 5 touch points (2 support, 3 resistance)
  2. Volume diminishes during formation
  3. Break above resistance with volume spike
  4. Price target ≈ wedge height

Bull Flag/Pennant

Sharp rally (pole) followed by downward-sloping consolidation (flag), then continuation upward.

Rules:
  1. Strong initial move on heavy volume
  2. Consolidation with declining volume
  3. Breakout in original trend direction
  4. Target ≈ pole height added to breakout

Market Structure Analysis

Understanding market structure helps identify trends, reversals, and key decision points where price is likely to react.

Uptrend

Characterized by higher highs (HH) and higher lows (HL). The trendline connects the swing lows.

Trading Rules:

  • Look for buy opportunities at trendline support
  • Enter after bullish reversal signals
  • Place stops below recent swing low
  • Take profits at previous resistance or fib extensions

Downtrend

Characterized by lower highs (LH) and lower lows (LL). The trendline connects the swing highs.

Trading Rules:

  • Look for sell opportunities at trendline resistance
  • Enter after bearish reversal signals
  • Place stops above recent swing high
  • Take profits at previous support or fib extensions

Break of Structure (BOS)

When price breaks a previous high in an uptrend or low in a downtrend, confirming trend continuation.

Trading Rules:

  • In uptrend: Price makes new high above previous HH
  • In downtrend: Price makes new low below previous LL
  • Wait for retest of broken level for confirmation
  • Trade in direction of break with tight stop

Change of Character (CHOCH)

Initial shift signaling potential trend reversal when price breaks previous swing low in uptrend or high in downtrend.

Trading Rules:

  • In uptrend: Price breaks previous HL making LL
  • In downtrend: Price breaks previous LH making HH
  • Requires confirmation (e.g., pattern, candle)
  • Use as confluence not standalone signal

Support and Resistance Trading

Support: Price level where buying interest is sufficiently strong to overcome selling pressure, causing the price to bounce upward.

Resistance: Price level where selling pressure overcomes buying pressure, causing the price to drop from that level.

Two Trading Approaches:

  1. The Bounce: Buy at support or sell at resistance with stop beyond the level
  2. The Break: Buy when price breaks above resistance or sell when breaks below support with stop on other side

Key Principles:

  • The more times price tests a level without breaking, the stronger it becomes
  • Broken support becomes resistance and vice versa
  • Watch for false breaks (stop hunts) before true breaks
  • Strongest levels align with multiple timeframes

Advanced Chart Patterns

Head and Shoulders

Type: Reversal (bearish)

Formation: Left shoulder (high), higher head, lower right shoulder, neckline support

Entry: Break below neckline

Target: Distance from head to neckline projected downward

Double Top

Type: Reversal (bearish)

Formation: Two similar highs with pullback in between, neckline support

Entry: Break below neckline

Target: Height of pattern projected downward

Double Bottom

Type: Reversal (bullish)

Formation: Two similar lows with rally in between, neckline resistance

Entry: Break above neckline

Target: Height of pattern projected upward

Fibonacci Trading Techniques

Fibonacci ratios occur naturally in financial markets and help identify potential reversal zones and profit targets.

Fibonacci Retracement

Drawn from swing low to high in uptrend (or high to low in downtrend) to identify potential pullback levels.

Key Levels:

  • 23.6% - Shallow pullback, trend remains strong
  • 38.2% - Moderate pullback
  • 50% - Common retracement level (not true Fib but widely watched)
  • 61.8% - Golden ratio, strongest support/resistance
  • 78.6% - Deep retracement, last chance before full reversal

How to Trade Retracements:

  1. Identify clear impulse move (swing)
  2. Draw Fib from start to end of impulse
  3. Watch for price action signals at key levels (61.8% most significant)
  4. Enter in direction of original trend with stop beyond Fib level
  5. First target at previous swing high/low

Fibonacci Extension

Drawn using three points (A-B-C) to project potential profit targets in the trend direction.

Key Levels:

  • 61.8% - Minimum expected target
  • 100% - Equal move to initial impulse
  • 138.2% - Extended target
  • 161.8% - Golden ratio extension

How to Use Extensions:

  1. Identify impulse wave (A to B)
  2. Wait for retracement (B to C)
  3. Draw Fib extension from A to B to C
  4. Take partial profits at 61.8% and 100%
  5. Let runners go to 161.8% with trailing stop

Combined Fibonacci Strategy

1

Identify Trend

Use higher timeframe to determine overall trend direction (weekly/daily charts)

2

Draw Retracement

On pullback, draw Fib retracement from swing low to high (uptrend) or high to low (downtrend)

3

Enter at Confluence

Look for entry at 61.8% or 78.6% retracement with price action confirmation (pin bar, engulfing, etc.)

4

Set Targets

Draw Fib extension for profit targets (61.8%, 100%, 161.8%)

5

Manage Trade

Take partial profits at 61.8%, move stop to breakeven, trail stop for remaining position

US Dollar Index Correlation

The US Dollar Index (DXY) measures the dollar's strength against a basket of currencies and correlates with many forex pairs.

DXY Composition

  • Euro (EUR) - 57.6% weight
  • Japanese yen (JPY) - 13.6% weight
  • British pound (GBP) - 11.9% weight
  • Canadian dollar (CAD) - 9.1% weight
  • Swedish krona (SEK) - 4.2% weight
  • Swiss franc (CHF) - 3.6% weight

Why It Matters

The US dollar is involved in about 85% of all forex transactions. Understanding DXY helps predict movements in:

  • EUR/USD (inverse correlation)
  • USD/CHF (direct correlation)
  • Gold (XAU/USD inverse correlation)
  • And many other USD pairs

Positive Correlation

Example: USD/CHF - When DXY rises, USD/CHF typically rises (and vice versa). This is because USD is the base currency in the pair.

Trading Tip:

Analyze DXY first when trading USD/CHF. If both show bullish structures, look for long opportunities in USD/CHF.

Negative Correlation

Example: EUR/USD - When DXY rises, EUR/USD typically falls (and vice versa). This is because USD is the quote currency in the pair.

Trading Tip:

If DXY is showing weakness with bearish structure, look for long opportunities in EUR/USD.

DXY Trading Strategy

1

Analyze DXY Structure

Determine if DXY is bullish (higher highs/lows) or bearish (lower highs/lows) on daily/weekly charts

2

Check Correlated Pairs

Identify EUR/USD, USD/CHF, and other USD pairs that correlate with DXY's direction

3

Find Confluence

Look for price action signals in the correlated pairs that align with DXY's trend

4

Execute Trade

Enter trades in direction of DXY trend when correlated pair shows confirmation

Price Action Trading Plan

1. Market Selection

  • Focus on 2-3 liquid currency pairs (e.g., EUR/USD, GBP/USD, XAU/USD)
  • Choose pairs with clear trends or defined ranges
  • Check economic calendar for high-impact news events

2. Timeframe Alignment

  • Determine trend on daily/weekly charts
  • Identify key levels on 4-hour chart
  • Fine-tune entries on 1-hour/15-minute charts

3. Trade Identification

  • Look for confluences: price action + structure + levels
  • Wait for clear signals (pin bars, engulfing, inside bars)
  • Measure risk/reward (minimum 1:2 ratio)

4. Trade Execution

  • Enter at confirmed signals with tight stops
  • Size position based on 1-2% risk per trade
  • Use limit orders at key levels when possible

5. Trade Management

  • Take partial profits at 1:1 risk/reward
  • Move stop to breakeven after favorable move
  • Trail stop to lock in profits

6. Post-Trade Review

  • Journal all trades with screenshots
  • Analyze both winners and losers
  • Refine strategy based on results

Mastering Price Action

Price action trading provides a pure way to analyze markets by focusing on what matters most - price movement itself. By combining the elements covered in this guide:

  • You'll learn to read candlestick patterns like a professional trader
  • You'll identify high-probability trade setups based on market structure
  • You'll use Fibonacci levels to find optimal entries and exits
  • You'll align your trades with higher timeframe trends
  • You'll understand how the US Dollar affects currency pairs

Remember: Price action mastery comes from screen time and practice. Start by focusing on 2-3 patterns or setups, master them, then gradually expand your repertoire.

Pro Tip:

Create a checklist of your trading rules and review it before every trade to maintain discipline.
