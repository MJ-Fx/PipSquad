1.1 What is Forex?

FOREX is the short form of Foreign Exchange. It's the global market that allows the exchange of one currency for another. The foreign exchange (FX) market, is the largest financial market in the world turning over more than $6.6 trillion a day.

Forex Trading is the buying and selling of currencies in the forex market with the aim of making a profit. The forex market is open 24 hours a day and 5 days a week, only closing down during weekends. Currency exchange rates are always fluctuating and this is where the money is made.

Practical Example:

Jack travels from Europe to U.S. with 5000 Euros. At departure, 1 Euro = 0.98 USD ‚Üí gets 4900 USD.

Returning a week later with 4900 USD when 1 Euro = 1.15 USD ‚Üí gets 5635 Euros.

Profit: 5635 - 5000 = 635 Euros gained from exchange rate fluctuations.

Note: Rates could also move against you, resulting in losses.

1.2 What is Traded in Forex?

Currencies

The primary forex instruments including majors (EUR/USD), minors (EUR/GBP), and exotics (USD/TRY).

Indices

Measure performance of stock groups like NASDAQ 100 (US100), S&P500 (US500), DAX (GER40).

Synthetics

Volatility indices and other synthetic instruments not affected by real-world events.

Commodities

Energy (Crude Oil), Metals (Gold, Silver), Softs (Coffee, Cocoa), Grains (Corn, Wheat).

Crypto

Cryptocurrency pairs like BTC/USD, ETH/EUR traded similarly to forex pairs.

1.3 Forex Market Participants

Federal Reserve (Fed), ECB, BoE - Influence rates and implement monetary policy

Commercial/Investment Banks

Hedge Funds

Connect to markets and trade smaller volumes

1.5 Essential Forex Terminology

Pip

Smallest price move (0.0001 for most pairs, 0.01 for JPY pairs)

Example: EUR/USD moves from 1.10500 to 1.10510 = 1 pip

Lot Size

Lot TypeUnits$/Pip
Standard100,000$10
Mini10,000$1
Micro1,000$0.10
Nano100$0.01

Leverage

Using borrowed capital (e.g., 1:100 means $1 controls $100 position)

Amplifies both profits AND losses

Bid/Ask/Spread

Bid: Price broker buys base currency

Ask: Price broker sells base currency

Spread: Difference between bid and ask

Long/Short

Long: Buy expecting price to rise

Short: Sell expecting price to fall

Margin

Collateral required to open/maintain position

Margin Call: Warning when equity falls below required margin

1.9 Forex Order Types

Market Order

Instant execution at current market price

Use when: You want immediate entry

Limit Order

Buy below market or sell above market

Use when: Expecting reversal at specific level

Example: EUR/USD at 1.2050, place sell limit at 1.2070 expecting rejection

Stop Order

Buy above market or sell below market

Use when: Wanting breakout confirmation

Example: GBP/USD at 1.5050, place buy stop at 1.5060 to catch upside momentum

Stop Loss

Automatically closes trade at specified loss level

Example: Buy EUR/USD at 1.2230 with stop at 1.2200 (30 pip risk)

Trailing Stop

Stop loss that follows price at specified distance

Example: Trail stop 30 pips behind price to lock in profits
